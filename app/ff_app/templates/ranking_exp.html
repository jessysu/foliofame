Ranking SP500 stocks

The stocks are compared to the peer stocks in the same period of time. During an arbitrary period of time, the top 12% of the stocks are ranked A. For S&P 500 stocks, the top 12% means the top 60 stocks. Then the A stocks are equally divided into 3 groups, A+, A and A-. Each of them has 20 stocks.

For S&P 500 stocks, the top 61-120 stocks belong to the group B. Similarly, top 121-180 stocks belong to group C, top 181-240 stocks belong to group D, and top 241-300 stocks belong to group E.

The lower 40% of the stocks are all ranked F.
